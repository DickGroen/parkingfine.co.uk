Know your fine

Private fine vs council PCN — a crucial difference

🟢 Private Parking Charge

Issued by NCP, Euro Car Parks, ParkingEye, APCOA, Smart Parking, etc.

  • ✓ Not a true fine — it’s an invoice
  • ✓ Cannot use bailiffs without a court order
  • ✓ Free appeal to POPLA or IAS
  • ✓ NtK timing rules often breached
  • ✓ Signage requirements frequently not met
  • ✓ Success rate significantly higher
🔴 Council PCN

Issued by local authority (Westminster, Manchester, etc.) or TfL.

  • — Official Penalty Charge Notice
  • — Can escalate to bailiffs if ignored
  • — Formal appeal to council, then adjudicator
  • ✓ 50% discount if appealed within 14 days
  • ✓ Grounds: error, mitigating circumstances, signage
  • ✓ Independent adjudicator free to use

Questions

Everything you need to know

What’s the difference between a private fine and a council PCN?
A council PCN is an official Penalty Charge Notice issued under the Traffic Management Act 2004 and can ultimately be enforced by bailiffs if ignored. A private parking charge is essentially an invoice from a private company — it cannot be enforced by bailiffs without first obtaining a County Court Judgment. Private fines are significantly easier to challenge.
What is a Notice to Keeper (NtK)?
A Notice to Keeper is sent to the registered keeper of the vehicle (rather than the driver) when the driver wasn’t identified at the time. For private companies, the NtK must arrive between 29 and 56 days after the alleged contravention. If it arrives outside this window, keeper liability under POFA 2012 may not be established — a strong appeal ground.
What if I ignore a private fine?
Don’t ignore it entirely. While private companies can’t send bailiffs directly, they can take you to the County Court for a CCJ — which would affect your credit rating. The best approach is to appeal formally, not to ignore. We provide the appeal letter to do this properly.
What is POPLA?
POPLA (Parking on Private Land Appeals) is a free, independent appeals service for private parking charges issued by BPA-member operators. If your appeal to the parking company fails, you can escalate to POPLA at no cost. Over 50% of POPLA appeals are decided in favour of the driver.
What if my fine is from the council?
Council PCNs have a formal appeal process. You must make an informal representation within 14 or 28 days (the notice will say which). If rejected, you can make a formal appeal, then escalate to an independent adjudicator free of charge. Appealing within 14 days also gives you a new discount window if the appeal fails.
Is this legal advice?
No. ParkingFine.co.uk provides an informational analysis and draft appeal letter. We don’t provide legal advice or represent you. For complex cases or court proceedings, consult Citizens Advice or a solicitor.
